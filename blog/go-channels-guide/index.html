Go Concurrency Channels

Introduction to Buffered Channels

Buffered channels in Go allow you to send values without a corresponding receiver. They're essential for building concurrent systems.

Buffered vs Unbuffered Channels

Unbuffered channels require sender and receiver to be ready simultaneously. Buffered channels decouple the sender and receiver in time.

Worker Pool Pattern

Buffered channels are perfect for implementing worker pools that process tasks concurrently with controlled parallelism.

Best Practices

Close channels from the sender side, use select for multiplexing, and always handle nil channels carefully to avoid panics.
